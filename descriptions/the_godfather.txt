The Godfather is an epic crime film that chronicles the Corleone family, a powerful and influential Italian-American crime family in New York City. The story, spanning from 1945 to 1955, centers on the transfer of power from the aging patriarch, Don Vito Corleone, to his reluctant youngest son, Michael. After an assassination attempt on his father, Michael, a decorated World War II veteran who initially wanted nothing to do with the family business, is drawn into the violent and ruthless world of the Mafia. He transforms from a naive outsider into a cunning and ruthless leader.
